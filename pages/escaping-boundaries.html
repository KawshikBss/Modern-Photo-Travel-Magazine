Escaping Boundaries

Experience is a thing that I never had the chance to gather plenty of, if I leave my academic life aside. Between my monotonous family life and packed academic schedules, I never was fortunate enough to undergo anything Bizarre. But something always has to fill the gap of something. Like “The Invisible Cities” I, Kublai Khan, Found my Marco Polo. Let’s give my Marco a name, let's call him Sam.
I have always been the Rupanzel, in her high castle, imagining the world and putting colour into it with her words, with no permission to see it in real life. His narration of different places, things and experiences, ignited my blank pages of experience. I learned to see the city of Dhaka from a totally new perspective, a way I never perceived or could ever perceive Dhaka. I could decorate my own tapestry of Dhaka with his strokes of experience and the colours of my imaginations. Who knew, just walking down a road can give you so many stories ? so many lenses to see things ? I learned to sense my vicinity through the chronicles of Sam. The best storyteller of my life. This person has this enchanting capability of turning everything into a story, mixing it up with imagination and surreal imagery.
I lived a multidimensional life through his stories. I’ve been to the beach and mountains at the same time, I’ve discovered myself as a bus driver riding to Italy, I found him fighting the crocodiles while I stood and screamed. We’ve been on a quest to find an island and declare it as a kingdom. The faint yellow night light of my congested room in a concrete jungle, used to turn into the magic carpet of Aladdin, and we used to fly through a million cities, that too through the tiny screen of our cellphones.
I remember the cheap bar of Italy, how we pretended to drink and laughed at each other's stupidity, I remember slow dancing on top of the London Bridge, climbing Everest, driving through a road full of foxes hanging from the tree branches, upside down. I still remember the day we went to Manila to eat ice cream, because it rhymes with vanilla. I didn’t even have my passport at that time. Also, the dark old zamindars palace, just to fish treasure from their ancient creepy pond. He forced me to dive to the bottom of the pond and find the box full of gold. I cannot even swim in real life. We even turned into trees the other day, me a mango tree and he, an innocent little jackfruit tree while we had a meaningful conversation from the perspective of trees.
Words are so powerful that just a conversation could create worlds, build cities, and make us travel the world within seconds. I haven’t been anywhere in the world other than the overcrowded, sleepless cities of Dhaka and Narayanganj. Yet, through our imaginations, I’ve been everywhere, I’ve my footprints all over the world. We made memories in places we’ve never been, the memories we can’t even say are not real.

Dola Saha
