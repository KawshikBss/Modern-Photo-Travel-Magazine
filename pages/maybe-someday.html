MAYBE SOMEDAY

"Within the intricate tapestry of Dhaka's streets, thousands of stories are whispered, stories that linger in the liminal spaces, unfinished and hidden in the shadows. Some tales falter, unable to bloom fully, stifled by the weight of sorrow and the haunting tendrils of fear. Yet, amidst these fragments, love endures as an ember that refuses to fade.

Much like Tridib in Amitav Ghosh's 'The Shadow Lines,' who wove an entire world within his being, it's conceivable that within the corners of his heart, certain fragments of his emotions yearned for May. Perhaps he imagined her presence in the quiet intimacy of a Kolkata tea stall that he cherished. There, in the midst of brewing chai and soft clinks of cups, his unspoken feelings for her found a sanctuary, becoming a part of the city's mosaic of hidden desires and uncharted connections."

Saif Mahmood
